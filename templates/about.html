About this site

Methodology, data sources, and what this site can and cannot tell you.

What is the Dáil Register of Members' Interests?

Every TD in Dáil Éireann is legally required to declare financial interests that could potentially influence their work as a legislator. The register is maintained by the Standards in Public Office Commission (SIPO) and published annually as a PDF.

TDs must declare interests in nine categories: occupations, shares, directorships, land and property, gifts, property or services supplied, travel facilities, remunerated positions, and contracts.

Despite being a public document, the register is effectively invisible. It is published as a PDF, with no search capability and no connection to any other public data. This site changes that.

How this site works

The site is built on three public data sources:

Oireachtas Register of Members' Interests All declared interests by category, for each TD data.oireachtas.ie ↗
Oireachtas Open Data API TD profiles, committee memberships, division (vote) records api.oireachtas.ie ↗
Companies Registration Office Company records, officer lists, filing history core.cro.ie ↗
Data pipeline

The register PDF is parsed using a text extraction tool (pdfplumber) that reconstructs the nine declared categories for each TD. The extracted data is then processed in three stages:

  1. Interest tagging: declared text is matched against a keyword taxonomy to assign sector labels (agriculture, property, finance, etc.)
  2. Conflict detection: sectors from declared interests are compared against sectors from committee memberships and voted-on legislation. Overlaps are flagged.
  3. CRO cross-check: declared company names are matched against CRO records to find dissolved companies, officer discrepancies, and companies not in the register.
Conflict methodology

A "conflict" in this site means: a TD holds a declared interest in a sector and either sits on a committee covering that sector, or voted on legislation in that sector.

Sector matching is keyword-based. For example, a TD declaring "Glanbia shares" is tagged as having a finance and agriculture interest; if they also sit on the Agriculture Committee or voted on a farm subsidy bill, that is flagged as an overlap.

Important: An overlap is not evidence of wrongdoing. TDs with farming backgrounds will naturally serve on agriculture committees and vote on agricultural legislation. This site presents the fact of the overlap and lets readers draw their own conclusions. It does not allege corruption, bias, or any impropriety.
What this site cannot tell you
  • The register relies on self-declaration. If a TD fails to declare something, and it is not in the CRO, this site cannot detect it.
  • Valuations of property, shares, or other assets are not required by the register — only descriptions. This site cannot determine the monetary value of declared interests.
  • This site covers only the 34th Dáil (from November 2024). Historical comparison across Dáils will be added in a future update.
  • Ministers have additional disclosure obligations under the Ethics in Public Office Act. Senator and Minister of State registers are not yet covered.
